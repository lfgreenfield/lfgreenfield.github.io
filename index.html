Web Mapping at the University of Kentucky

My name is Laura Greenfield and the following portfolio of projects shares my introductory work to web-mapping. The work I aim to engage in is Nonprofit GIS, where the skills and platforms enabled by geospatial technology are used to help grassroots and community organizations. This field is important and necessary because mapping products can be used to increase awareness of social issues, as well as support logistics management operations of organizations. Collaborations thusfar have either been completed in a volunteer capacity or through engagement in a course at the University of Kentucky.

Nonprofit GIS: Step by Step collaboration

This map, in its development phase, is the start of a project for Step By Step, a community organization in Lexington focused on connecting young mothers (ages 14-24) to services. The map is part of a service mapping movement which aims to provide geospatial services based on the needs of a community organization.

Final functioning map features will include an address search box, info panel displaying attributes for each service, and an ability to filter services according to service category.
